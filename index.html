    • Facts about the brain

    Weight The brain weighs about three pounds. Fat The brain is the fattiest organ in the body, with at least 60% of its dry weight made up of fat. Pain The brain doesn't feel pain, even though it's the command center for pain perception in the rest of the body. This allows for brain surgery to be performed while the patient is awake. New cells The brain continues to produce new neurons throughout life, a process called neurogenesis. Blood vessels The brain has 100,000 miles of blood vessels, which is enough to circle the earth four times. Oxygen The brain uses 20% of the oxygen in your body, which is three times as much as your muscles use. Brain cells start to die after five minutes without oxygen. Exercise Aerobic exercise increases blood flow to the brain, which is good for it.
